Cloud Computing Definition

On-demand delivery of IT resources which you pay only for what you use

History of AWS

2003 - Paper by 2 guys about SQS
2004 – Launch of SQS
2006 – Launch of AWS
2007 – Launch of EC2 and 108,000 developers signed on
2012 – First Re:Invent Conference
2013 – Introduction of certifications
2020 – Renewable energy to power all AWS data centers worldwide

Architecture

17 Regions worldwide, each with a minimum of 2 Availability Zones, each AZ with a minimum of 2 data centers

Business Challenges in IT

Budget, time to market, scalability, infra management, security and compliance

Compute

Elastic Cloud Compute (EC2)

On-demand: compute resources with per second billing
Spot instances: bidding on instances based on supply/demand based spot price, gives 2 minute prior notification before stopping an instance for someone with a higher bid
Reserved instances: full upfront, partial upfront, and no upfront payment options for 1 and 3 year contract durations
Dedicated Host: you rent the underlying hardware, single-tenancy model

Storage

Simple Storage Service (S3)

Standard: 99.999999999% durability, 99.99% availability
Infrequent Access: 99.999999999% durability, 99.99% availability
Reduced Redundancy: 99.99% durability, 99.99% availability
For data that can be reproduced
Glacier: 99.999999999% durability, 99.99% availability
for archiving, takes 3-5 hours to retrieve data
